# Design Context

## Users

A mix of three overlapping readers:

- **Future-me.** Posts work like public notes — a place to think out loud. The tone can be unpolished and exploratory.
- **Tech peers.** Devs who land on a post from search or social. They expect real code, real reasoning, no marketing fluff.
- **Recruiters / hiring managers.** Will skim the about/resume page and glance at recent posts. Needs to read as serious and competent without tipping into corporate.

Reading context: a browser tab opened during a work break, a phone read on a commute, a late-night follow-through on something someone linked. Not a user who's spending 20 minutes on the site — a user who's deciding in 10 seconds whether to keep reading.

The site has to work in all three modes without picking a winner.

## Brand Personality

**Warm + handmade + a little weird.**

Voice: personal, direct, slightly self-deprecating. Writes like a person, not a brand. Comfortable being uncertain in public.

Feels like: a hand-stitched zine. A letter typed on an old mechanical keyboard, kept as a keepsake. A fabric tag sewn into a jacket pocket. Handwritten notes in the margin of a library book. A pamphlet someone made themselves and hands you at a reading.

Does NOT feel like: a product. A personal brand. A funnel.

Emotions the interface should evoke: curiosity, slowness, a small smile at an unexpected detail.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Overall style :**  minimal, content-forward, date-stamped list, no-chrome confidence.

**Anti-references (explicit):**

1. **Corporate dev blog** (Vercel, Cloudflare, Stripe). Gradient accents, glossy hero illustrations, engineer-as-influencer energy. We go the opposite way.
2. **VitePress / Nuxt docs default.** The "every Vue project" look — sidebar, search bar, sterile neutral palette. We must not be mistaken for a documentation site.
3. **Dev portfolio template.** Big hero, animated blobs, "skills" card grid, "Let's build something amazing" CTA. None of this.

**Theme:** Light by default (paper-like, comfortable for long reading). Dark mode available via visible toggle for late-night readers — tinted warm dark, not a generic zinc-900 palette.

**Typographic direction** (to commit to in the craft pass):
- Display face: something with character — a soft slab, a quirky serif, a humanist display face. Not Fraunces / Newsreader / Cormorant / Playfair. Look further.
- Body face: a paired face that feels written-by-a-person. Possibly a well-drawn grotesk or humanist sans. Avoid Inter / Plus Jakarta / Instrument Sans.
- Mono for code: something with warmth (e.g. Commit Mono, Monaspace, Berkeley Mono if licensed, or a catalog discovery). Avoid IBM Plex Mono and Space Mono.

**Color direction:**
- Warm off-white paper background (OKLCH, not pure white)
- Ink color — deep warm brown-black, never `#000`
- Single accent color pulled from a physical-object palette (think: faded terracotta, library-card red, inkwell teal). One accent, not a ramp.
- Neutrals tinted subtly toward the accent hue for cohesion.

**Spatial direction:**
- Asymmetric layout. Left-aligned, not centered.
- Deliberate irregularity: not every gap is a multiple of 8. A handmade object has slightly off measurements; the page should too.
- Wide reading measure (~65ch) with generous line-height. Long-form-first.

## Design Principles

1. **Content is the chrome.** Dates, titles, and prose do the visual work. Ornamentation only appears where it *is* the content.
2. **One detail worth pointing at.** Something small and specific that gives the site personality — not a gimmick, a seam that shows the site was made. (Decide in craft pass.)
3. **Nothing shouts.** Low contrast in hierarchy, high contrast between content and surface. Earned emphasis only.
4. **Slightly irregular beats perfectly regular.** Deliberate asymmetry, off-grid spacing, hand-measured feel — never algorithmic.
5. **No templates.** If an element could be lifted from a Vue starter or a Medium post, rewrite it from the content up.
